Investment Restrictions and Prohibitions for Foreign Investors in China's General Aviation and Low-Altitude Economy Sectors

Supported by policies, technology, and market factors, various sectors within China's low-altitude economy are experiencing vigorous development. Based on the Special Administrative Measures for Foreign Investment Access (Negative List) (2024 Edition) (Promulgated by Order No. 23 of the National Development and Reform Commission and the Ministry of Commerce on September 6, 2024, effective November 1, 2024), the restrictions and prohibitions on foreign investment in China's general aviation and related low-altitude economy sectors are summarized as follows.

## I. General Aviation Sector

### (A) Restrictions on Corporate Control
- **Legal Representative Qualification**: The legal representative of any general aviation company must be a Chinese citizen (Item 9).

### (B) Foreign Equity Ratio Restrictions
- General aviation companies for agriculture, forestry, and fishing must be established as joint ventures.
- Other general aviation companies must be Chinese-controlled (i.e., foreign shareholding ratio less than 50%) (Item 9).

### (C) Prohibited Businesses for Foreign Investment
- General aviation operation services are not explicitly prohibited but must comply with control requirements. Sensitive areas (e.g., military applications or national security) may trigger additional reviews.

## II. Low-Altitude Economy Related Sectors

### (A) Airport Construction and Operation
1. Civil airport construction and operation must be under Chinese relative control. Foreign parties cannot participate in airport control tower construction or operation (Item 10).
2. Implication: Low-altitude flight activities highly depend on airport infrastructure. Foreign investors cannot obtain key resources by controlling airports.

### (B) Aircraft Manufacturing and Maintenance
- The Negative List does not explicitly restrict foreign investment in aircraft manufacturing or maintenance.
- The Encouraged Catalogue for Foreign Investment Industries (2022 Edition) encourages cooperation in high-end manufacturing.

### (C) Drones and Low-Altitude Services
1. **Information Transmission and Data Processing**: Foreign equity in Value-Added Telecom Services shall not exceed 50% (Item 12).
2. **Geographic Information Data**: Foreign investment is prohibited in geodetic surveying, marine charting, aerial photogrammetry for surveying and mapping, ground mobile surveying, and navigation electronic map compilation (Item 19).

## III. Other Relevant Restrictions

1. **Security and Regulatory Requirements**: Sectors involving national security (e.g., aircraft airworthiness certification, airspace management) may require passing a security review.
2. **Industry Qualifications and Licenses**: Foreign investors need to apply for a General Aviation Operating License through a Chinese-controlled or joint venture entity.

## Summary

China adopts a policy of **limited openness + strict control** regarding foreign investment in general aviation and low-altitude economy:

| Sector | Restriction |
|--------|------------|
| **General Aviation** | Chinese control (<50% foreign equity); Chinese legal representative; JV for agri/forestry/fishery |
| **Airports & Infrastructure** | Chinese relative control; no foreign participation in control towers |
| **Technology & Services** | VAS ≤50% foreign equity; prohibited in surveying/mapping data |

Foreign investors can participate through joint ventures or cooperative models but must ensure compliance with Chinese control requirements and strictly adhere to national security and industry regulatory rules.
